# Key Design Decisions

A log of the 4 biggest engineering calls made on Arka and the reasoning behind each.

## 1. AISI 4130 Chromoly over Mild Steel
Higher cost, harder to source. Chosen for ~2× yield strength, better fatigue life under 
cyclic race loads, and clean TIG weld behaviour. Enabled thinner walls at the same safety 
factor — net mass saving justified the premium.

## 2. E-glass over Carbon Fibre
Carbon fibre at 5–10× the cost with no autoclave = not realistic for a student team on a 
tight budget. Body is non-structural. E-glass meets the brief: light, stiff enough to hold 
aero shape, hand-layup compatible.

## 3. Steel Mould over CNC Foam Mould
CNC machining of a 5m polyurethane foam mould is expensive and hard to source in Delhi. 
Laser-cut steel cross-sections welded into a box frame with a skin sheet achieved a functional 
mould at ~1/5th the cost. Introduced thermal distortion and weight challenges that were 
solved on-site.

## 4. Non-Structural Body over Monocoque
A monocoque would integrate body and structure but is far harder to manufacture without 
precision tooling. Separating the spaceframe (structural) from the composite body 
(aerodynamic shell) simplified fabrication significantly given our 3-week timeline.
